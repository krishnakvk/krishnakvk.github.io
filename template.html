About 

 
  In 2006, Amazon Web Services (AWS) began offering IT infrastructure services 
  to businesses in the form of web services -- now commonly known as cloud computing. 
  One of the key benefits of cloud computing is the opportunity to replace up-front 
  capital infrastructure expenses with low variable costs that scale with your business.
  With the Cloud, businesses no longer need to plan for and procure servers and other 
  IT infrastructure weeks or months in advance. Instead, they can instantly spin up 
  hundreds or thousands of servers in minutes and deliver results faster.
